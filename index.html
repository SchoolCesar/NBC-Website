About the Conference

The National Bankruptcy Conference (NBC) was formed in the 1930s when leading bankruptcy scholars and practitioners began meeting informally at the request of Congress. Their work helped draft major Depression-era bankruptcy law amendments, including the Chandler Act of 1938
The Conference was formally established in the 1940s and has served as a trusted resource to Congress on every significant piece of bankruptcy legislation since that time

Current Members

Membership in the National Bankruptcy Conference is by invitation only. Its approximately 60 members include leading bankruptcy scholars, federal judges, and practitioners from major law firms across the United States
