Providing Non-Partisan Expertise on Bankruptcy Law

The National Bankruptcy Conference is an invitation-only organization of leading scholars, judges, and practitioners dedicated to advising Congress on the improvement of bankruptcy and insolvency law in the United States.


About the Conference

Founded in the 1930s at the request of Congress, the National Bankruptcy Conference has played a central role in shaping modern bankruptcy law, including contributions to major legislative reforms and amendments.

The Conference does not advocate for business or political interests. Instead, it provides thoughtful, consensus-based recommendations grounded in deep professional experience.

Our Work

Legislative Recommendations

Formal letters and reports submitted to Congress regarding bankruptcy reform proposals.

Policy Analysis

Detailed evaluations of current insolvency issues and emerging legal developments.

Historical Contributions

Participation in drafting and refining major bankruptcy legislation over decades.

Membership

Membership is limited and by invitation only. Members include distinguished bankruptcy judges, leading academics, and experienced practitioners from across the United States.

For inquiries related to legislative matters, research, or media requests, please contact the Conference through its official communication channels.
